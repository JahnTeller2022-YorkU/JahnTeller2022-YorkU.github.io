General information

The Jahn-Teller Effect was first proposed by Jahn, Teller, and Landau in 1930s. In 1950s, Opik and Pryce supplemented it with the pseudo-Jahn-Teller Effect. Being the only sources of spontaneous symmetry breaking in polyatomic systems, the Jahn-Teller and pseudo-Jahn-Teller Effects induce ubiquitous phenomena in molecules and solids. In a broader context, JTE and PJTE are symmetry-induced vibronic interactions. Such interactions between vibrational and electronic degress of freedom determine properties of many materials. In this virtual symposium, we invite speakers to present their cutting edge scientific works on vibronic interactions. This is a multidisciplinary symposium for physicist, chemists, and material scientists. It covers topics in experimental observations, materials designing, computational modellings, and pure theoretical developments in vibronic interactions. The symposium provides the best opportunity to exchange ideas about the latest advances in this field.



Topics of the Symposium

  • Spectroscopic studies of vibronic coherence.
  • Vibronic interactions in core-valence excitations.
  • Latest advances in simulating vibronic spectra and non-adiabatic dynamics.
  • Interplay between electronic spin and vibronic interactions.
  • Vibronic interactions and quantum information science.
